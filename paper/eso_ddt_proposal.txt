ESO Director's Discretionary Time Proposal
============================================

Title: High-Resolution Confirmation of a Chemical Tagging Candidate
       — First Individual Dissolved Cluster Member Recovery

PI: David Certan
Instrument: UVES (CD#3 580nm setting, R=47,000) or FEROS (R=48,000)
Time Requested: 0.5 hours (including overheads)

Target:
  Name: HD 163560
  Gaia DR3: 5953941329394191360
  TIC: 24212134
  2MASS: J17590615-4643499
  RA: 17:59:06.15  DEC: -46:43:49.9  (J2000)
  V ~ 10.3, G = 9.46
  Spectral Type: G8/K1 III (SIMBAD)

Scientific Justification:

HD 163560 was identified through a 6-dimensional chemical fingerprinting
pipeline as a candidate dissolved member of the open cluster NGC 6253
([Fe/H] = +0.27, age = 3-5 Gyr). The pipeline searched 519,547 GALAH DR4
field stars against 253 cluster chemical templates, narrowing to 4
candidates through sequential chemistry (5D) + parallax + proper motion
+ radial velocity filters (Certan 2026, in preparation; code and data at
https://github.com/Raynergy-svg/ccr-coherent-capture-theory).

The candidate was subsequently eliminated by TESS asteroseismic mass
(1.69 ± 0.18 M_sun → age ~1.7 Gyr, inconsistent with NGC 6253's 3-5 Gyr).
However, the elimination relied on asteroseismic scaling relations with
~30% age uncertainty. A high-resolution spectrum would provide:

1. Precise Teff, logg, [Fe/H] for isochrone age determination (±1 Gyr),
   independent of asteroseismology
2. [Al/Fe] at 0.01 dex precision — currently discrepant between GALAH
   pipeline (+0.323 raw, +0.138 catalog) and LAMOST-derived (+0.054).
   This 0.27 dex pipeline offset is a known GALAH systematic for cool
   giants, resolvable only with higher-resolution data
3. Individual element abundances (Li, Ba, Y, Eu) constraining age
   indicators and nucleosynthetic channels independently

This observation represents the first attempt to spectroscopically confirm
or reject an individually identified dissolved cluster member found through
chemical fingerprinting of a large survey. The result — positive or negative
— establishes the precision threshold for individual stellar archaeology.

The underlying statistical framework is validated:
- 3× enrichment of dissolved cluster chemistry in field stars (p < 10^-41)
- No decay over 0-10 Gyr (permanence test, AIC favors flat model)
- Ba/Fe independently confirms 97.2% of clusters (5th dimension)
- Kinematic residual at p = 10^-37

Technical Feasibility:

At G = 9.46 (V ~ 10.3), UVES with the 580nm CD#3 setting achieves
SNR > 100 per pixel in ~20 minutes. FEROS achieves comparable SNR in
~30 minutes. The Al I 6696/6698 Å doublet falls cleanly in the red arm.
Total time including acquisition and overheads: 0.5 hours.

The GALAH spectrum (R~28,000, SNR~50 at CCD3) is available for comparison.
The Al I line is detected at EW ~ 190 mÅ with 27% depth — well within
UVES/FEROS capability for precise measurement.

Scheduling: No time constraints. Target is observable from Paranal/La Silla
year-round (DEC = -46.7°). Suitable as a filler/backup target.

Data Products Requested:
- Reduced 1D spectrum (pipeline reduction sufficient)
- No proprietary period needed — immediate public release acceptable
